    John intends to meet with this traitor tonight. It is my heartbreak that I must betray such a good and kind master, but I must believe the rebels will win. Perhaps, with my intervention, I might buy my master some sort of help when the war is over. But that is thinking too far ahead.
    My serving dish empty, so I scoot back into the kitchen for more. When I return, Master John is gone but I glimpse the corner of Arnold’s jacket disappearing out the door. I must hurry to listen in. What gems can I discover for my people, for the cause? Arnold is the one who’s rotten, not John. My information will help the rebels see this.
    I duck back into the kitchen and drop my serving dish off once more. Quickly, I run through the servant’s stairs toward John’s room. There is a door there that I can hide behind, press my ear against, and listen. I will write down the details later. There is nothing I cannot remember.
    My breath hitching, I stop to remove my shoes at the top step. I shuffle through the darkness, holding my skirts tight. The men are talking, of military strategies and ideas that I don’t fully comprehend. Yet I know that men wiser than me will value these tidbits. I all but hold my breath. My master speaks kindly to this terrible man, the one that wishes so much harm upon my new nation.
    I hear murmurings of “West Point.” The phrase is oft-repeated. I note it in my mind. Is it just me, or is the light burning brighter… the voices getting closer?
    Suddenly, the door is ripped open from within. Arnold glowers down at me and snarls, “I knew it! A spy!”
