Union Stockyards, Chicago 1890

The Great Union Stock Yards of Chicago surpassed the country, and at times even the world, in their output of butchered meat. As this aerial view reveals, the pens for various livestock stretched far across the landscape, as did the chambers for the slaughter and preparation of the meat.

The Yards, 1897

This photograph overlooking the Union Stock Yards offers another glimpse of the massive complex, which consumed a swath of the city nearly one square mile in size.

The Jungle first edition cover

Upton Sinclair’s landmark The Jungle changed the narrative of the conditions in these Chicago stock yards and forever secured a place for the ardent muckraking journalist in uncovering the truth in modern American culture.

Floorers removing the hides, Union Stock Yards, Chicago” Sueling & Koehn Co.

The processing of livestock in the Union Stock Yards required a similar process not that unlike Ford’s assembly line. Here, men work to remove hides from slaughtered animals in preparation for their butchering.

International Live Stock Exposition Catalogue Cover

The Chicago Union Stock Yards was known for its livestock processing, but it was also a leading source of those wishing to buy livestock in the Midwest. This catalogue cover is from one of the livestock expositions held on the stock yard grounds.
