Fei Hong was a simple man. He worked wood during the day and returned home to his family in the evenings. He was dedicated to his craft, but no matter how hard he worked he couldn’t seem to get ahead. This just wasn’t because of a weak economy or money mismanagement; it was simply how life was. Each day he toiled and sawed as much as he could before returning home, but it was never enough.He wanted to give his family the sort of life he couldn’t afford. Servants, finery from the West, and so many other wonderful things. Little did Fei Hong realize that opportunity was about to come knocking.
Little did Fei Hong realize that opportunity was about to come knocking. One night after a long day at work, he was walking home and came across two men carrying boxes. One was large and strong but the other looked as if the wind might blow him over. Exhausted but ever kind, Fei Hong lifted the heavy box from the delicate man and carried it for him.
After the delivery, Fei Hong was paid handsomely for his work. In fact, more than twice his typical daily wage. Confused, he asked what it was they were carrying. In response, the scrawny man offered him a handful of black, powdery goop. Fei Hong threw it to the ground, his heart racing. “Opium?” he gasped. “You let me carry opium?!”
“We help the foreigners smuggle it into the city. The elite buy it by the cask and we are paid very well for our work,” smirked the weak man. “If you ever decide you want a job, let us know.”
Fei Hong hurried away from the drug smugglers and went home with their money in his pocket. Perplexed, he said little to his son and his wife that evening and went to bed early. With money like this, he could afford all the things his family deserved. But he was ignorant of the drug world and only knew it worked in the shadows, and the dark is a frightening place.
