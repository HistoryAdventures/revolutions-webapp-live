Diamonds

Natural diamond crystal embedded in kimberlite, from Finsch Diamond Mine, South Africa

  • Diamonds are probably best known for the sparkle that they add to jewelry, but they are also essential for industrial trades. Considered one of the hardest naturally occurring gemstones, diamonds have become essential in a variety of polishing and cutting applications.
  • Even today the diamond mining trade thrives in Africa. This is particularly the case in Botswana, which is home to two of the world’s largest diamond mines. In 2018, Botswana exported $5.9 billion in diamonds, which reflected 4.3% of the world’s total exported trade in diamonds.
