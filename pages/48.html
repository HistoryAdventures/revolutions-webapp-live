History of Cong o Free State

The rush among European powers in the later nineteenth century to assume control of the African continent meant that many Africans, like Khari, were faced with brutal conditions and even more vicious attacks by those forces seeking to dominate his people. From the degradation of land to boost crops to the violent outbursts of Belgian King Leopold II’s Force Publique, the rapid shift in the African landscape resulted in the difficult choices with which warriors like Khari were faced.
