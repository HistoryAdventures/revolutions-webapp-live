JIEMBA

Death oF the rainbow serpent

   According to the calendar of the white invaders, the year is 1795. That is not, though, how we measure time. It is not how we know our world, but they do not care. I sit in the sacred space near the great blue bay. My people, the Eora Nation, have called these lands home for thousands of years. Since the time that we were given the animals, the water, and the lands by our creator gods and the rainbow serpent, we have remained in our home.
   Now, we face a challenge that we cannot survive. White men from across the sea invade bringing with them sickness and aggression.
   My Biyanga, the man who raised me, saw the first white men land seven years ago. They came to this very bay on the back of great wooden ships with sails larger than anything he had ever seen. Biyanga had not seen whites before, though he had heard whispers of them before. Their ships had been spotted off the coast some years before.
   At first, our people thought these pale people were the ghosts of our ancestors. Clearly, though, these were not the ghosts of those who came before us. “Our ancestors respected the land. These white men do not. No, these white men come to claim our animals, our land, and even our people for themselves. Because of them, my Biyanga is no longer here. The man who raised me died from the illness they brought. These changes that they bring are unwelcome, yet when we fight back we are slain in huge numbers. We do not possess their weapons, those new and terrible dangers that they have brought with them. How can such a small object create so much death so quickly?
   We do not suffer alone. As their sicknesses take us, so do their animals’ illnesses take our hunting game and the predators that hunt with us. Beyond this, they kill every predator they come across to protect their animals. They do not understand that the predator is as important as the prey; or perhaps more so. The predator shows you where to drink and takes you to the game trail. The prey simply are.
   But these invaders have no use for the predator. They kill without reason, without mercy, be it animal or human. They see us as no better than the common kangaroo. It is our animals that they stare at, and we do the same
